I woke up this morning and went to the inclusive breakfast. I then sat down
and pondered. Shall I stay? Shall I go? I checked the weather and it said
"rain" but none of the hourly reports said "rain" and as it turned out it did
not rain but nonetheless this factored into my decision to stay - as did
having "too much" time to get to Verona and the possibility that rest might
actually be good after 11 days of cycling. The hostel is also great and the
extraordinarily cheap, ensuite, private room is wonderful.

The responsible man told me the previous evening that, should I wish to stay
another night, I should book on
Hostelworld and save some money. I tried this but the Hostel did not show up.
I asked the responsible lady of the current morning in English "Hi, can I book
another night?" she looked at me and in broken English indicated that it's not
possible, I tried to tease more information out of her by trying to explain,
in German, that the responsible man had said that there was plenty of
availability but she had already picked up the phone and then she put down the
phone and she hammered away at the computer then told me "you can stay, but
the system doesn't work at the moment" "It's no problem?" "No it's fine, you
pay later".

So my breakfast was eaten and I did some computer things and spoke to some
computer people then I decided I should go for a run. I picked the first
running route that Strava generated and sent it to my watch. I then detached
the bottom half of my convertible trousers to make shorts and, with my
"barefoot" shoes, started out on a 10k run. Slowly at first before finding my
pace.

I hadn't done any "barefoot" runs for some months and although it wasn't
painful when running, after 45 minutes when I stopped to change my music I
could feel my calves were burning and they'd be very very tender for the
remainder of the day. Fortunately it was also cool enough that I didn't sweat
excessively and therefore "ruin" my t-shirt. The cleanliness of my clothes is
a scarce resource as I have precisely:

- Convertible trousers
- Running t-shirt (doubles as normal t-shirt)
- Long-sleeved top
- Hawaii shirt
- 2 x socks and 2 x pants (1 of each is out-of-service now).

I wanted to hand-wash things today but I forgot to steal the little soaps and
shower gels from the hotel in Konstanz, the hostel doesn't provide any and I'm
not going to buy a large container or get a dirty bar of soap that's going to
soapify everything in my washbag. Maybe I'll get a chance in Innsbruck.

One hour was spent rehearsing my talk for PHPDay - it came in at 44 minutes
and the slot is 50 minutes so it's just about right.

An unproportionate amount of time was spent adding an [overview page for my
bicycle tours](/tour/verona26/) with a map that aggregates all the GPX tracks
into one map.

Tomorrows plans were laid down today and the plan is to:

- Cycle 135k to Insbruck.
- Book into one of the youth hostels there (there are multiple).
- Eat pizza and drink beer.
- Do the Innsbruck [Parkrun](https://www.parkrun.co.at/innpromenade/) at 9am.
- Cycle over the 2211m [Penser Joch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penser_Joch) pass to
  Bolzano.

Beyond that it would be a matter of a days ride to Trento and from there to
Verona. I might get a haircut.
